BIOGRAPHY

Dr. Alexa Pichet Binette obtained her master’s degree in clinical neuroscience at the Charité Universitätsmedizin, Berlin, Germany and her PhD in neuroscience at McGill University, Montreal, Canada under the supervision of Professor Sylvia Villeneuve. Her thesis focussed on identifying brain imaging and behavior changes related to preclinical Alzheimer’s disease. In 2021 she joined the Clinical Memory Research Unit at Lund University, Sweden where she completed her postdoctoral training under the supervision of Professor Oskar Hansson. Her research uses multimodal neuroimaging techniques, fluid biomarkers and cognitive assessments to better understand disease progression.

INTEREST

Her research aims to better understand aging and neurocognitive disorders, particularly Alzheimer's disease, with the goal of preventing or slowing the progression of these conditions and their degenerative effects. To achieve this, the laboratory she leads employs multimodal approaches to biomarker analysis, integrating various types of neuroimaging (PET and MRI), blood markers, and proteomics across local and international cohorts of participants and patients. Her research program is funded by the Fonds de recherche du Québec and the IUGM Foundation.

WithinCRIUGM, she works closely with cognition clinics to bridge clinical research and practice, enabling access to cutting-edge biomarkers that help establish more precise diagnoses and prognoses.
