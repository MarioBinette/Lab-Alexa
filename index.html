PICHET BINETTE LAB

We are currently seeking new students. If you are interested in pursuing a Master's or Ph.D. in the field of neuroscience, we would be delighted to meet with you. We encourage you to explore our website and assess whether our research aligns with your academic aspirations. Should you find a match, please do not hesitate to contact us.

Our laboratory investigates the biological mechanisms underlying aging and neurodegeneration, with a primary focus on Alzheimer’s disease.

We employ a comprehensive, multimodal approach that integrates neuroimaging techniques (MRI and PET), blood-based biomarkers, and proteomic analyses to examine cognitive decline and identify early indicators of Alzheimer’s disease. A central focus of our research is pTau—a critical biomarker intimately linked to the disease’s progression. Utilizing longitudinal cohort studies alongside advanced machine learning methodologies, we strive to enhance early diagnostic capabilities and explore robust intervention strategies. By uniting expertise in neuroscience, imaging, and artificial intelligence, our mission is to deepen our understanding of brain aging and contribute meaningfully to the prevention and treatment of neurodegenerative disorders.
