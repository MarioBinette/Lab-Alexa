APOE4 impact on soluble and insoluble tau pathology is mostly influenced by amyloid-beta
APOE4 is the strongest genetic risk factor for Alzheimer's disease. While it's linked to amyloid-beta (Aβ) plaques, its relationship with tau accumulation is unclear. Studies on three cohorts showed Aβ, not APOE4, is the main predictor of tau accumulation.
